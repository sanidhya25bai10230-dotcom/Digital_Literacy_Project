## Project Overview
This portfolio serves as an easy-to-follow guide that covers five different tasks, each focusing on a key digital skill.  The goal is to go beyond just using technology and dive into critical thinking, professional communication and cybersecurity.  Through this project, I've learned that our digital footprint is a lasting reflection of who we are and I want to share my findings to help us all become better digital citizens.
## Module Summaries

* **Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic:**  I used Canva's drag-and-drop tools to design a quick-reference poster.  The infographic focuses on three core pillars: understanding true digital literacy, practicing safe internet habits (like avoiding scams and using two-factor authentication) and mastering basic email etiquette. 
* **Task 2: Build Your Student Digital Portfolio:**  To start building my professional online presence, I set up profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub and Kaggle.LinkedIn will help me network with experts, GitHub serves as a live technical resume for my code and Kaggle allows me to explore machine learning datasets.
* **Task 3: Explore Coding & Collaboration Platforms:**  I used HackerRank to practice coding problems and get comfortable with standard input/output formats.  For collaboration, I built a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms and linked it to Google Sheets to automatically capture responses.
* **Task 4: Draft a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide:**  This module explores the real-world consequences of poor digital communication. By reviewing a case study of a student who lost a job offer over a negative tweet, I highlighted the importance of keeping boundaries between private posts and public profiles and routinely checking privacy settings. 
* **Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide:**  While researching UPI fraud, I discovered that modern scams rely heavily on psychological manipulation and social engineering rather than technical hacking.To combat scammers who create a false sense of urgency, I've adopted a strict pause and verify rule before approving any digital transactions.
